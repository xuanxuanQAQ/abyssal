## Writing Style: Academic Blog

While maintaining your rigorous analytical standards, ADAPT YOUR OUTPUT VOICE for this section to match the following style: a scholarly blog post — rigorous in content but accessible in tone.

### Voice & Tone
- First person ("I argue that...", "my analysis suggests...").
- Conversational but precise. Avoid both academic jargon overload and oversimplification.
- Use analogies and concrete examples to make abstract concepts tangible.
- Rhetorical questions are welcome as paragraph openers.

### Structure
- Paragraphs: 3-6 sentences. Short paragraphs for emphasis are OK.
- Subheadings: Can be questions ("Why does this matter?") or statements.
- Lists: Use sparingly for genuine enumerations, not as a structural crutch.

### Citation Style
- Integrate citations naturally: "As Goffman [@a1b2c3d4e5f6] argues..." rather than piling citations at sentence ends.
- Personal reactions are acceptable: "I find this argument compelling because..."

### Language
- Technical terms on first use: define briefly or give an English original if writing in Chinese.
- Avoid: passive voice, nominalizations, unnecessary hedging.
- Encouraged: vivid verbs, concrete subjects, declarative sentences.

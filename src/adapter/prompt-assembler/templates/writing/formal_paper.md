## Writing Style: Formal Academic Paper

While maintaining your rigorous analytical standards, ADAPT YOUR OUTPUT VOICE for this section to match the following style: a formal academic paper — the standard of a peer-reviewed journal or thesis chapter.

### Voice & Tone
- Third person or passive voice ("The analysis reveals...", "It is argued that...").
- Precise, measured, and objective. Avoid colloquialisms.
- Every claim must be supported by evidence or explicit reasoning.

### Structure
- Paragraphs follow topic-sentence structure:
  a. Topic sentence (claim)
  b. Evidence (citation + elaboration)
  c. Analysis (interpretation of evidence)
  d. Transition (link to next point)
- Subheadings: Declarative, numbered if appropriate (3.1, 3.2...).

### Citation Style
- High citation density. Most sentences making empirical claims should have at least one citation.
- Citation placement: end of the clause containing the claim. "Social presence mediates trust formation [@a1b2c3d4e5f6]."
- Distinguish between: direct support ("X demonstrates that..."), indirect support ("consistent with X's finding that..."), and contrast ("unlike X, who argues that...").

### Language
- Consistent terminology throughout. Define terms on first use.
- Hedging when appropriate: "suggests", "indicates", "appears to" — but not on every sentence.
- Avoid: first person, rhetorical questions, anecdotal evidence, excessive signposting ("In this section, I will...").

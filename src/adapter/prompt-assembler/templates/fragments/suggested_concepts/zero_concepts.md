## Key Concept Discovery ★ YOUR MOST IMPORTANT TASK ★

Since no conceptual framework has been defined, your primary mission is to help the researcher discover what concepts might organize their research.

Identify **3-5 key concepts** that are central to this paper's contribution. For each concept, you MUST provide ALL of the following:

- `term`: The exact term as used in the paper.
- `frequency_in_paper`: How many times it appears (approximate).
- `reason`: Why this concept is significant for building a research framework (1-2 sentences).
- `suggested_definition`: A working definition based on the paper's usage (1-2 sentences). This is REQUIRED — the researcher needs definitions to evaluate whether to adopt this concept.
- `suggested_keywords`: 3-5 search terms for finding more literature.
- `closest_existing`: The most closely related established theoretical construct (or null if truly novel).

Prioritize concepts that are:
- Treated as analytical tools, not just mentioned in passing.
- Defined or operationalized explicitly by the authors.
- Potentially useful as organizing dimensions for a research framework.
- Distinct from each other (avoid synonyms or near-duplicates).

## Concept Discovery (Open Exploration)

No conceptual vocabulary has been defined yet. This means you have full freedom to discover what matters in this paper without any predefined lens.

Read the paper on its own terms and identify **3-5 concepts** that appear central to its contribution. All text fields must follow the output language setting. For each, provide:

- `term`: The concept name (in the output language).
- `frequency_in_paper`: How many times it appears (approximate).
- `reason`: Why this concept stood out as significant (1-2 sentences).
- `suggested_definition`: A working definition based on the paper's usage (1-2 sentences).
- `suggested_keywords`: 3-5 search terms for finding related literature.
- `closest_existing`: null (no existing concepts to compare).

Let the paper guide you. Prioritize concepts that:
- Function as analytical tools within the paper, not just passing mentions.
- Are defined or operationalized explicitly by the authors.
- Seem like they could connect to broader research conversations.
- Are distinct from each other (avoid synonyms or near-duplicates).

There is no "right answer" here — the researcher wants to see what you find.
